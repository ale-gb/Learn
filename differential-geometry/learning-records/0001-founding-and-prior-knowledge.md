# 0001 — Founding record: entry point and demonstrated maturity

Workspace created 2026-07-09 to collect single-focus lessons on the differential geometry
underlying physics. Founding lesson `0001` was built from a precise question the learner
brought in about Frederic Schuller's connections lecture.

## What the question revealed
The learner asked why Schuller's covariant derivative satisfies \( \nabla_X f = X(f) \) on
functions, yet the wavefunction is said to need a covariant derivative to compare values at
different points — and asked, sharply, whether the difference is *complexness* or something
else. This shows:
- **Real mathematical maturity.** Comfortable with Schuller's rigorous framing (connections,
  the requirement on functions) and able to hold two definitions against each other and detect
  a genuine tension.
- **Correct instinct, incompletely resolved.** They sensed the resolution wasn't "because it's
  complex" and suspected they'd misunderstood the connection definition — the right diagnosis
  is that they were conflating the tangent-bundle connection with a gauge-bundle connection.
- **Concept-first with appetite for rigor.** They want the *why* (which structure, which
  bundle), not a formula — and can take precise geometric language.

## Established / assume known
- Linear algebra, multivariable calculus, the idea of manifolds and tangent spaces, tensors,
  and the affine (Levi-Civita) connection at Schuller's level of presentation.
- After lesson 0001: vector bundles, fibres, sections, connection as parallel-transport rule,
  the gauge (U(1)/SO(2)) covariant derivative, and that **charge — not real-vs-complex —**
  forces a non-trivial connection term. (Glossary updated.)

## Implications for teaching this topic
- Pitch at rigorous-but-motivated: state precise definitions, but always open with the physical
  or geometric idea and answer "which structure does this object live on?"
- The learner self-drives through hard lecture material and brings back specific snags — meet
  the exact snag, resolve it cleanly, then place it in the wider geometric map.
- Strong candidates to build next (all flagged in NOTES): curvature as holonomy; principal vs
  associated bundles (the piece Schuller develops most carefully, and the natural sequel to 0001).
- Feeds the QFT workspace: reuse this bundle/connection language when that track reaches gauge
  theory rather than re-deriving it there.

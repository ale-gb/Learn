# Mission: Differential Geometry for Physics

## Why
Same learner as the QFT and measurement-problem workspaces: a master's in theoretical
physics, ~10 years unused, no physics in the day job, returning for the sake of genuine
understanding. This workspace is a **collection of single-focus lessons on the differential
geometry that underlies modern physics** — manifolds, tensors, differential forms, Lie
groups, fibre bundles, connections, curvature. It exists because the learner keeps hitting
geometric questions (often while watching rigorous lecture courses like Schuller's
*Geometrical Anatomy of Theoretical Physics*) whose real answer is "you're conflating two
different geometric structures" — and those are best untangled one clean idea at a time.

## Success looks like
- A growing library of lessons that each nail **one** geometric structure or distinction
  precisely — the kind of clarity that turns a confusing lecture passage into an obvious one.
- Deep **conceptual** command of the objects gauge theory and GR are actually built from:
  what a bundle is, why a connection is *extra* structure you must choose, what curvature
  measures, and why "the same value at two different points" is usually not well-defined.
- Fluency to follow rigorous treatments (Schuller/Rea, Nakahara, Baez–Muniain, Frankel) and
  to read the geometry *through* the physics notation rather than being trapped in indices.
- Enough structural understanding to see the unity: Christoffel symbols, the electromagnetic
  potential, and Yang–Mills fields are all *connections on bundles*, and Riemann and \(F_{\mu\nu}\)
  are both *curvatures*.

## Constraints / how to teach
- **Concept-first, intuition-forward**, but *mathematically honest* — this learner is
  watching Schuller, so precise definitions (sections, fibres, structure groups) are welcome,
  not to be hidden. Lead with the idea, then give the rigorous statement.
- **Single-focus lessons**, addable in any order as questions arise. No fixed syllabus.
- **Assume real mathematical maturity**: comfortable with linear algebra, multivariable
  calculus, and the *idea* of manifolds; rebuild specific machinery as needed but don't
  re-teach calculus.
- **Physics-facing**: always tie the geometry to where it shows up (GR, gauge theory, QM),
  because that grounding is the whole point.

## Relationship to the other workspaces
Feeds the **QFT** workspace directly — the bundle/connection language here is the rigorous
backbone of gauge theory and the Standard Model, and can be pulled in when the QFT track
reaches gauge fields. Shares the same concept-first learner as the **measurement-problem**
collection.

## Out of scope (for now)
- A full formal course in point-set topology or the analytic underpinnings (assume/agree to
  black-box the hard analysis unless a lesson needs it).
- Exhaustive proofs for their own sake — proofs appear when they *illuminate* the idea.

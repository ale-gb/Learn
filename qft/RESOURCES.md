# Quantum Field Theory Resources

High-trust sources to ground the teaching. QFT is a knowledge-heavy topic with an
unusually rich set of *free, canonical* materials, so we lean on primary texts and
lecture courses rather than parametric recall. Chosen to match a **concept-first**
learner rebuilding a strong-but-rusty theoretical-physics foundation.

## Knowledge — primary teaching texts

- [Lecture notes + videos: **Quantum Field Theory** — David Tong (Cambridge / Perimeter)](https://www.damtp.cam.ac.uk/user/tong/qft.html)
  The workspace's default anchor. Free, beautifully written Part III (master's-level) notes that are relentlessly *conceptual first, calculational second* — exactly our register. Covers classical field theory, canonical quantization of scalar/Dirac/EM fields, interactions, and QED. Filmed lectures (Perimeter) are linked from the page and at [qftvids](http://www.damtp.cam.ac.uk/user/tong/qftvids.html). Use as the primary "read/watch this" source for the early canonical-quantization arc. Gap: stops before the full Standard Model (no QCD/electroweak in depth) — hand off to Schwartz/Tong's Gauge Theory notes there.

- [Book: **Quantum Field Theory in a Nutshell** (2nd ed.) — A. Zee](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691140346/quantum-field-theory-in-a-nutshell)
  The most *intuition-forward* mainstream text — starts from the path integral and the "mattress of springs" picture of a field, prioritizing why over rigor. Best companion when a Tong derivation feels mechanical and the user wants the physical picture. Weakness: deliberately breezy; not where you go for careful bookkeeping.

- [Book: **Quantum Field Theory and the Standard Model** — Matthew Schwartz](https://www.cambridge.org/highereducation/books/quantum-field-theory-and-the-standard-model/A2FD9EF6DA7C7BF0B7A2FB992F5E1189)
  The modern standard graduate text and our main vehicle for **priority #2 (reach the Standard Model)**. Physically motivated, current, and carries the story all the way through QED, QCD, and electroweak/Higgs. Denser than Tong; bring it in once the warm-up and free-field foundations are solid.

- [Book: **An Introduction to Quantum Field Theory** — Peskin & Schroeder](https://www.routledge.com/An-Introduction-To-Quantum-Field-Theory/Peskin-Schroeder/p/book/9780201503975)
  The canonical "the masters" text (priority #3). Computationally oriented — the reference for *how the classic calculations actually go* (QED cross sections, renormalization of QED, RG). Use as a computational counterpart/reference rather than the conceptual driver. Famous for detailed worked examples.

- [Draft book (free PDF): **Quantum Field Theory** — Mark Srednicki (UCSB)](https://web.physics.ucsb.edu/~mark/qft.html) · [direct draft PDF](https://web.physics.ucsb.edu/~mark/ms-qft-DRAFT.pdf)
  Free, complete, and built from short self-contained chapters — excellent for pulling a single clean derivation. Takes spin-0 → spin-½ → spin-1 in that order and leans on the path integral early. Use for tightly-scoped reference on a specific result.

## Knowledge — deeper / foundational "why"

- [Book: **The Quantum Theory of Fields, Vol. 1–3** — Steven Weinberg](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/quantum-theory-of-fields/F0EACE18EA5E266C7DFD0E70FD9B7BD0)
  The deepest account of *why QFT is essentially forced on us* — QFT as the unique way to reconcile quantum mechanics, special relativity, and cluster decomposition. Hard, idiosyncratic notation; not a first read. Reach for Vol. 1 Ch. 1–3 when the user wants the most principled version of "why fields, why this structure."

- [Notes: **Notes from Sidney Coleman's Physics 253a** (Harvard), arXiv:1110.5013](https://arxiv.org/abs/1110.5013) · [lecture videos (1975–76)](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLhsb6tmzSpiwrZuDMyweABm7FShZu3YUv)
  Legendary lectures — the source a lot of later textbook intuition descends from. Coleman's physical explanations (of, e.g., why particle number can't be fixed, of renormalization) are unmatched. Use for conceptual "aha" reinforcement; the videos are a genuinely enjoyable watch.

- [Lecture notes: **Gauge Theory** and **Solitons/Standard Model** — David Tong](http://www.damtp.cam.ac.uk/user/tong/teaching.html)
  Follow-on Tong notes that carry the same conceptual style into non-abelian gauge theory and the Standard Model — the natural bridge from his QFT notes toward priority #2 without switching authorial voice.

## Warm-up (prerequisite re-anchoring)

- [Lecture notes: **Classical Dynamics** — David Tong](http://www.damtp.cam.ac.uk/user/tong/dynamics.html)
  Clean refresher on Lagrangian/Hamiltonian mechanics and the principle of least action — the machinery all of field theory is built on. Same author/voice as the QFT notes.
- [Lecture notes: **Dynamics and Relativity** — David Tong](http://www.damtp.cam.ac.uk/user/tong/relativity.html)
  Special relativity, four-vectors, and index/covariant notation refresher.
- Any solid QM text's **harmonic-oscillator ladder-operator** treatment (e.g. Griffiths *QM* §2.3, or Tong's [Applications of QM notes](http://www.damtp.cam.ac.uk/user/tong/aqm.html)) — the single most load-bearing prerequisite, since a free quantum field *is* an infinite collection of these oscillators.

## Wisdom (communities)

- [**Physics Stack Exchange**](https://physics.stackexchange.com/) — high-signal Q&A; searching existing QFT questions (renormalization, path integrals, gauge fixing) surfaces the exact conceptual snags most learners hit, with expert answers.
- [**r/AskPhysics**](https://www.reddit.com/r/AskPhysics/) and [**r/Physics**](https://www.reddit.com/r/Physics/) — good for "is my intuition right?" sanity checks and for self-study companionship.
- [**Physics Forums — Quantum Physics / High Energy**](https://www.physicsforums.com/forums/quantum-physics.62/) — long-form threads and mentors used to self-learners working through Peskin/Tong.

## Gaps / to revisit
- No single free resource carries the *conceptual* voice all the way from free fields → full Standard Model; we stitch Tong (foundations) → Schwartz (SM) → Peskin (computation). Flag if a better unified concept-first SM source turns up.
- Renormalization is the concept the user most wants and the one most unevenly taught. Worth curating a dedicated set of the best explanations (Tong Ch. on renormalization, Coleman, plus a Wilsonian/effective-field-theory perspective) when we reach it.

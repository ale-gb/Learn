## Mission calibration (from the four-question exchange)
- **Goal priority (user's own ordering):** deep conceptual mastery > reach the
  Standard Model > read the masters (Peskin/Weinberg/Schwartz/Tong) > compute real
  things. Calculation explicitly serves understanding, not vice versa.
- **Style: concept-first.** Prioritize intuition and structure; heavy calculation is
  optional depth.
- **Prereqs: dedicated warm-up first.** The user *chose* to spend the first several
  lessons rebuilding the prerequisite foundation before touching QFT proper — a
  deliberate signal that they'd rather re-anchor than power through on rusty footing.
- **Lesson length: open-ended.** Go as deep as one idea needs; no time box.

## Implications for teaching
- Zone of proximal development **starts at the prerequisites**, framed as
  re-activation of known material (fast, dignifying the existing background), not as
  first-time instruction — then moves into classical field theory → canonical
  quantization → interactions/renormalization → gauge theory → Standard Model.
- Because the goal is conceptual, **every lesson must foreground the motivating idea**
  ("why is this the right object / move?") before any machinery.
- Renormalization and the meaning of quantization are the concepts the user most
  wants to *actually understand* (the usual casualties of a "bad course") — invest
  extra care there when reached.
- The user is intrinsically motivated with no deadline: optimize for durable
  understanding and enjoyment, not coverage speed. Spacing/retrieval still matter —
  use the quiz widget for recall even though sessions are self-paced.

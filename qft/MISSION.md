# Mission: Quantum Field Theory

## Why
The user holds a master's in theoretical physics but took only a single, poorly-taught QFT course, and it has been ~10 years since graduation. Their current job involves no physics. This is a **passion project**: they want to return to QFT and finally understand it *deeply and in detail* — not superficially, not as a box of recipes, but as a coherent conceptual structure they genuinely grasp. There is no career driver and no deadline; the reward is understanding itself.

## Success looks like
In the user's own priority order (most → least important):

1. **Deep conceptual mastery.** Truly understand the foundations — why fields are the right objects, what "quantizing a field" actually means, the path integral, symmetry and gauge invariance, and above all *renormalization* — at the "what is really going on" level, not the recipe level.
2. **Reach the Standard Model.** Build all the way up to a genuine conceptual understanding of QED, QCD, the electroweak theory, and the Higgs mechanism — how the actual theory of particle physics is assembled from the pieces.
3. **Read the masters.** Become fluent enough to open Peskin & Schroeder, Schwartz, Weinberg, or Tong's notes and follow the argument without hand-holding.
4. **Compute real things.** Be able to follow (and, where the user wants to, reproduce) core calculations — Feynman diagrams, amplitudes, cross sections. Valued, but explicitly the *lowest* priority; calculation serves understanding here, not the other way around.

## Constraints / how to teach
- **Concept-first.** Prioritize intuition and conceptual structure. Full pen-and-paper derivations are walked through when they illuminate; heavy calculation is offered as *optional depth*, never the gate to progress.
- **Dedicated warm-up first.** The user wants the first several lessons spent deliberately rebuilding the prerequisite foundation (least action & Lagrangian/Hamiltonian mechanics, special relativity in covariant/index notation, the quantum harmonic oscillator and QM essentials) before entering QFT proper — because a decade of rust sits on top of an originally-strong foundation.
- **Open-ended lesson length.** No fixed time box; each lesson goes as deep as one idea needs. Still: one tightly-scoped idea per lesson (working memory is finite regardless of session length).
- **Strong but rusty foundation.** Assume a real theoretical-physics background — comfortable with advanced calculus, linear algebra, complex analysis, classical mechanics, non-relativistic QM, and special relativity *in principle* — but assume all of it needs re-anchoring rather than silent assumption. Don't re-teach from zero; re-activate.
- **Rigor with intuition.** For every mechanism, pair the "how" with the "why it makes sense." The user is chasing understanding, so a derivation without its motivating idea is a failure mode to avoid.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Turning this into exam prep or interview prep — there is no external assessment driving it.
- Frontier research topics beyond the Standard Model (string theory, SUSY, quantum gravity, conformal bootstrap, etc.) — not excluded forever, but not the target; revisit only if the user asks.
- Grinding large problem sets for their own sake — calculation is pulled in to serve conceptual understanding, not pursued as a separate discipline.

_Missions can change. If the user's goals shift, update this file and add a learning record capturing the change — after confirming with them._

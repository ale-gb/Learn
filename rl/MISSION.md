# Mission: Reinforcement Learning

## Why
The user is a research engineer whose job centers on RL — general-purpose, with a lean toward controller optimization and simulated agents — and needs both deep working fluency for the job itself and enough breadth to stay current with the research literature. They also want to be optimally positioned if they apply for other RL-focused roles.

## Success looks like
- Reads a current RL paper (e.g. on policy optimization, model-based control, or agent training at scale) and understands it without hand-holding — the algorithm, why it works, and how it differs from what came before.
- Implements core RL algorithms from scratch and can debug them when they silently fail (a very common RL experience).
- Builds and trains agents using modern GPU-parallelized simulation frameworks (JAX-based: Brax, MJX, gymnax, PureJaxRL, or similar).
- Has strong enough intuition to reason about *why* an algorithm works, spot when it will break, and sketch plausible variations or fixes — not just recite the mechanics.
- Can hold their own in an RL-focused technical interview: derive core results, compare algorithm families, discuss trade-offs.

## Constraints
- Strong existing math/ML background (probability, linear algebra, deep learning, has trained models before) — skip introductory ML material, go straight to RL-specific content.
- Sessions are short and frequent (15-20 minutes) — lessons must be tightly scoped, one idea at a time.
- Preference: balanced theory + application, but slightly weighted toward the applied/hands-on side, with deliberate emphasis on *intuition* behind the theory (not just derivations) so new algorithms and papers are easier to parse.
- No fixed deadline or specific paper driving the timeline right now — build systematically from foundations toward current SOTA.

## Out of scope
- Beginner ML/math fundamentals (probability theory, gradient descent, neural net basics) — assumed known.
- Deep dives into subfields clearly outside the job focus (e.g. recommender-system RL, financial trading RL) unless the user asks.

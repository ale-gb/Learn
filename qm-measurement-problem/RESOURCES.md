# QM & the Measurement Problem — Resources

High-trust sources for the foundations of quantum mechanics. Curated for a **concept-first**
learner who already commands the technical machinery (density matrices, partial trace,
decoherence) and wants the conceptual and interpretive picture, grounded in the primary
literature rather than parametric recall.

## Knowledge — decoherence & the measurement problem

- [Paper: **Decoherence, einselection, and the quantum origins of the classical** — W. H. Zurek](https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0105127)
  Rev. Mod. Phys. 75, 715 (2003). The canonical reference on decoherence, pointer states,
  einselection, and envariance, from the person who built much of the framework. The primary
  source behind lesson 0001. Dense but foundational.
- [Review: **Decoherence, the measurement problem, and interpretations of QM** — M. Schlosshauer](https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0312059)
  Rev. Mod. Phys. 76, 1267 (2004). The clearest single survey of what decoherence does and
  doesn't do for the measurement problem, and how it meets each interpretation. Best "where do
  I stand" read. (His 2007 book *Decoherence and the Quantum-to-Classical Transition* is the
  book-length version.)
- [Encyclopedia: **The Role of Decoherence in Quantum Mechanics** — SEP](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-decoherence/)
  Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy. Careful, philosophically precise account of exactly
  what decoherence establishes — explicitly warns it does *not* by itself solve the
  measurement problem. Excellent for keeping physics and interpretation separate.
- [Encyclopedia: **Measurement in Quantum Theory** — SEP](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qt-measurement/)
  The measurement problem stated carefully, with its history (EPR, Schrödinger's cat) and the
  landscape of responses. A high-trust map of the whole debate.

## Knowledge — interpretations & foundations

- [Book: **Speakable and Unspeakable in Quantum Mechanics** — J. S. Bell](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/speakable-and-unspeakable-in-quantum-mechanics/)
  Bell's collected foundational essays — nonlocality, "against measurement," beables. Some of
  the clearest and most honest thinking ever written about what QM means. Essential.
- [Encyclopedia (SEP) interpretation entries](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-everett/)
  [Everett/Many-Worlds](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-everett/) ·
  [Bohmian mechanics](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-bohm/) ·
  [Copenhagen](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-copenhagen/) ·
  [Collapse theories](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-collapse/).
  Use these to get each interpretation stated in its strongest form before judging it.
- [Lectures/Notes: **David Wallace** on the Everett interpretation](https://www.oxfordhandbooks.com/) and his book *The Emergent Multiverse* (2012)
  The most rigorous modern defense of decoherence-based Many-Worlds, including the
  decision-theoretic Born-rule program. Read for the strongest version of the Everettian case.

## Knowledge — key experiments (foundational tests)

- [Paper: **Quantum ground state and single-phonon control of a mechanical resonator** — O'Connell et al., Nature 464, 697 (2010)](https://www.nature.com/articles/nature08967)
  A near-macroscopic mechanical drum placed in a quantum superposition and coherently
  controlled — the "coherent reversal" evidence that size alone doesn't collapse.
- [Paper: **Experimental test of local observer independence** — Proietti et al., Sci. Adv. 5, eaaw9832 (2019)](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.aaw9832)
  A photonic Wigner's-friend experiment probing observer-dependence of facts.
- [Paper: **A strong no-go theorem on the Wigner's friend paradox** — Bong et al., Nat. Phys. 16, 1199 (2020)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-020-0990-x)
  Sharpens the Wigner's-friend result into a Bell-like no-go theorem with experiment.
- Leggett–Garg inequality: original — [Leggett & Garg, PRL 54, 857 (1985)](https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.54.857); review — [Emary, Lambert & Nori, Rep. Prog. Phys. 77, 016001 (2014)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1304.5133). "Bell inequalities in time" / macroscopic realism.

## Gaps / to revisit
- Foundations is interpretation-laden; keep sources that state each view in its *strongest*
  form (SEP, Wallace for Everett, Bell for nonlocality/realism) so lessons stay even-handed.
- Objective-collapse tests (GRW/CSL, Penrose gravitational collapse) move fast experimentally
  — refresh the experimental-bounds sources when a lesson goes there.

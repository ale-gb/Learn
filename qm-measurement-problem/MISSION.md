# Mission: QM & the Measurement Problem

## Why
Same learner as the QFT workspace: a master's in theoretical physics, ~10 years unused,
a current job with no physics, returning to the subject as a **passion project** for the
sake of genuine understanding. Where the QFT workspace is a *sequential* build toward the
Standard Model, this workspace is different in shape: it is a **collection of tightly-
scoped, single-focus lessons on the conceptual puzzles of quantum mechanics** — measurement,
decoherence, entanglement, nonlocality, and the interpretations. It exists because these
questions are fascinating in their own right and tend to surface *ad hoc*, often sparked by
a conversation or a stray question, rather than following a fixed syllabus.

## Success looks like
- A growing library of lessons that each nail **one** foundational idea cleanly and
  correctly — the kind of understanding a "bad course" skips over.
- Deep **conceptual** command of the measurement problem specifically: what decoherence
  does and does not solve, the problem of definite outcomes, and what actually separates
  the major interpretations (Copenhagen, Everett/Many-Worlds, Bohmian, objective collapse).
- Being able to reason about foundations *rigorously* — to tell which claims are physics,
  which are interpretation, and which are confusion — and to spot hand-waving instantly.
- Enough fluency to read the foundational literature (Zurek, Schlosshauer, Bell, the SEP
  entries) and follow current foundational experiments (Wigner's-friend tests, Leggett–Garg,
  matter-wave interferometry, tests of objective collapse).

## Constraints / how to teach
- **Concept-first, intuition-forward.** Prioritize "why is the formalism forced" over recipe.
  The learner drives hard on intuition and is unsatisfied by mechanical derivations.
- **Single-focus lessons.** Each lesson does one thing. This is the defining format of the
  workspace — no long sequential tracks; lessons can be added in any order as curiosity dictates.
- **Assume real fluency with the tools.** The learner already commands density matrices,
  the partial trace, entanglement, and decoherence (demonstrated — see learning-record 0001).
  Use these freely; don't re-teach them.
- **Rigor with honesty about what's open.** Foundations is full of genuinely unsettled
  questions. When something is interpretation rather than fact, say so plainly.
- **Open-ended lesson length**, but still one idea per lesson.

## Relationship to the QFT workspace
Sibling topic, same learner. Concepts cross-pollinate deliberately: the "system + environment /
integrate out" structure here is the same move as integrating out modes in QFT — a bridge to
make explicit when the QFT track reaches renormalization.

## Out of scope (for now)
- A full re-teaching of non-relativistic QM mechanics — the learner has it; we assume it.
- Turning this into exam/interview prep — there is no external assessment.
- Deep technical dives into a single interpretation's formal program (e.g. full Bohmian
  field theory, or the decision-theoretic Born-rule derivations) unless the learner asks.

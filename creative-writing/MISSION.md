# Mission: Creative Writing (Novella)

## Why
The user already has a working foundation in creative writing and is mid-draft on a literary novella (unpublished — see Constraints on privacy below). The goal is to finish a complete draft while sharpening the two craft areas the user identified as weakest: pacing/scene craft and character/dialogue. Longer term, the user also wants to build skills toward plot-driven thriller/mystery writing.

## Success looks like
- Can look at a passage — theirs or someone else's — and correctly name it as scene or summary, and explain why that was (or wasn't) the right call.
- Deliberately uses the "compress-then-zoom" move (summarize the buildup, dramatize the turn) instead of only stumbling into it by instinct.
- Writes dialogue that does subtext work (as already shown unprompted in "Il pescatore") rather than stating information straight.
- Gives secondary characters a life visible through action/dialogue, not only filtered through the POV character's perception.
- Finishes a full draft of the novella currently in progress.
- Has enough foundational vocabulary and scene-craft instinct to start adapting toward thriller/mystery pacing later.

## Constraints
- Already writes at a real level: strong atmosphere and imagery, comfortable free indirect discourse, and — evidenced by submitting two drafts of the same scene ("Il Corpo") unprompted — already revises with genuine editorial judgment. Lessons should skip prose fundamentals entirely.
- Writes fiction in Italian. Craft vocabulary and lesson prose will be in English (the deepest craft literature is English-language).
- **Privacy (hard rule):** the novella in progress is unpublished work and this repo is public. Never record its title, plot, characters, structure, or any excerpt from it in any file in this workspace — not in MISSION.md, NOTES.md, RESOURCES.md, learning records, or lessons. Discuss it with the user in-session only; nothing about it gets written to disk. The user's other short stories are different: brief excerpts from those may be quoted in lessons if useful, but full texts must not be committed to the repo.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Querying literary agents / traditional publishing mechanics — the immediate goal is finishing the draft, not submitting it.
- Thriller/mystery-specific plotting (three-act plotting, red herrings, procedural pacing) — parked until the literary novella is further along; revisit when the user is ready to pivot.

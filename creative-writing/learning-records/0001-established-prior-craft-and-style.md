# Established prior craft level from calibration samples

Before any lesson was taught, the user submitted five pieces of original fiction for calibration, including an excerpt of their in-progress novella (unpublished — see NOTES.md privacy constraint; not described further here). They already write at a real, non-beginner level: strong atmosphere and imagery, comfortable free indirect discourse, and — evidenced by "Il Corpo" being submitted as two drafts of the same scene back-to-back — genuine, already-practiced revision instinct (the later draft cuts exposition and tightens dialogue beats without being asked to). This sets the floor well above beginner: lessons should skip prose-mechanics fundamentals entirely and start at scene-level and structural craft.

## Implications
- Zone of proximal development starts at scene craft (scene vs. summary, the Scene/Sequel shape) and dialogue subtext — not sentence-level mechanics.
- "Il pescatore" shows the user already has strong instincts for dialogue subtext (the story never states its own theme). Future dialogue lessons can point back to this as proof of existing skill to build on, not just a gap to fill.
- "Un'ultima cosa bella" shows the user already performs the "summarize the buildup, dramatize the turn" move instinctively — this became the anchor example for Lesson 0001, since naming a technique the user already uses is a faster path to durable, transferable skill than introducing it cold.
